Designing the Brolly App

iOS Design / UI Design / UX Design

As the vision for Brolly became clearer, it also became clear that the app in its current form was not delivering in terms of UX or features. We had a desire to make the app more intuitive, showcase insights, deliver joy and reward those who fill in questions.

PROJECT GOAL

Redesign the app with the target of showcasing insights, being intuitive and truly “smart”. This design will be the top goal that any smaller UI elements will build up into this singular vision.

APPROACH

Design Process

Whilst working within the fast-paced startup environment, we focused on using a collaborative, user-centered and iterative process to take this project from ideation, to design and development. Team collaboration and collaborative design were critical not just in the early stages of idea generation but also alignment of the vision and decision making. We used user data (from Mixpanel), user feedback (collated from a variety of sources) and ongoing feedback to help craft our new experience.

DISCOVER

Affinity Mapping

Across a period of two weeks we arranged calls with users to discuss their wider problems and misunderstandings with insurance. We then sat down and went through our notes together and created post-its filled with insight from our users. These insights were then categorised into: Problems, Emotions and Desires. We further grouped similar post-its in order to highlight the most prominent issues (even giving them catchy titles!)

Key findings: Users didn't care for insights, only actionable insights. They wanted to be rewarded for loyalty (not punished)

DISCOVER

Competitor Analysis

Some competitor analysis was conducted with both companies directly related to Brolly’s business model as well as indirect competitors, we looked at information structure, layout, tone and navigation.

Key findings: Range of tones are useful for conveying emotions/importance. Gamified apps provide immediate reward through pop-ups

DISCOVER

User Feedback

As the person responsible for UX I am constantly logging user feedback and this came quite useful when the time came to redesign the app. Taking a look at the Trello board I can see what features are most requested, as well as problems people are having.

Key findings: Users wanted more competitive quotes and a clearer understanding of what they are covered for

CREATE

UX / Wireframing

Developing the UX through wireframes is a great way to visualise the information and patterns we have collected so far, and begin to piece together the experience and interface. The key stakeholders and myself would review the wireframes frequently until we settled on and agreeable layout. I found that discussions over rough wireframes are much more insightful, creative and useful than discussions over fully designed UI as it allows stakeholders to be more open in their feedback.

CREATE

Design System

It is equally important to build a good infrastructure behind any app design. It was my priority at Brolly to build a system that would enable cohesive design across all platforms. As well as defining components this also included added new colours to our limited palette, defining our typographic scale. The extra colours would allow more variety and depth to the app, whereas the clearly defined typographic scale would allow us to create clearer hierarchies. This was all built within Sketch utilising the Symbols and Text Styles features.

CREATE

App Flow

Alongside creating the wireframes it is important to understand the flow of the app, how the features piece together to create a good user experience - we did this by creating a flow diagram of the different sections of the app and showing how they interconnected.

CREATE

Interface Design

Once the wireframes had been decided upon and the design vision was narrowed, I began to design the UI elements in Sketch.

From talking to users, the feedback we received on what they’d like to see on the home screen was mixed. But primarily it was split between two different priorities, viewing ‘my policies’ and receiving ‘insights on my cover’. The way we decided to tackle this was by utilising the home screen as a dashboard, whereby the user is presented with easily-digestible pieces of information that then directs them down their desired path.

Here we see the Policies in a greater detail, in the previous app version you would have to click into the policy to see any information - but by displaying them as cards we can include the most vital pieces of information at a higher level. They also carry the same visual aesthetic as the home screen so we continue the familiarity across app. We also included and option to sort between expiring soonest/latest and most/least expensive.

Something new that hadn’t been explored in the previous app was the inclusion of a profile. In our desire to gamify and reward the user in a Brolly experience, we considered it vital to have somewhere in the app that would be the centrepiece of this: the profile. We placed the profile completeness bar high up and with greater prominence because the more complete a user’s profile, the better quotes that Brolly can automatically retrieve for them (and quote forms filled indirectly). The user will also see an overview of their covered assets, the green tick signifying it’s insured, the green ring signifying it is “complete”

One of the questions we posed during the redesign of the app was how do we reward users for answering questions, and particularly get them to answer questions outside of a quote form. The solution we decided upon was to a small number of easy-to-answer questions on the homescreen that would open as a popup and display immediate gratification upon answering.
